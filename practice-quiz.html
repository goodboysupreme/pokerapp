Section A — Taxation & Incidence (Ch 19)

1. A flat-rate income tax imposes 25% on all income over $25,000 (the first $25,000 is untaxed). For a taxpayer earning $80,000, the tax due is:

Correct: $13,750. Taxable income = $80,000 − $25,000 = $55,000. Tax = 0.25 × $55,000 = $13,750. The $20,000 distractor applies 25% to the full $80k; $18,750 applies it to $75k.

2. Under the same tax structure, the average tax rate at an income of $45,000 is:

Correct: 11.11%. Tax due = 0.25 × ($45,000 − $25,000) = $5,000. Avg rate = $5,000 ÷ $45,000 × 100 = 11.11%. Note 7.14% is the rate at $35k; 14.58% is at $60k.

3. Looking at the full schedule, this tax is best classified as:

Correct: Progressive. The marginal rate is flat at 25%, but the average rate rises from 0% to 18.75% as income grows. A flat marginal rate with an exempt bracket creates progressivity through the average rate.

4. Market demand is \(Q_d = 8 - \tfrac{1}{3}P\) and supply is \(Q_s = P - 8\). The pre-tax equilibrium price is:

Correct: P = 12. Set \(Q_d = Q_s\): \(8 - \tfrac{1}{3}P = P - 8 \Rightarrow 16 = \tfrac{4}{3}P \Rightarrow P = 12\). Then \(Q = 12 - 8 = 4\).

5. An excise tax shifts supply to \(Q_s = P - 12\). The new equilibrium price paid by consumers is:

Correct: P = 15. \(8 - \tfrac{1}{3}P = P - 12 \Rightarrow 20 = \tfrac{4}{3}P \Rightarrow P = 15\). New quantity \(Q = 15 - 12 = 3\).

6. Based on the shift from \(Q_s = P - 8\) to \(Q_s = P - 12\), the per-unit excise tax equals:

Correct: 4. The P-intercept of the supply equation shifts from 8 to 12 — a gap of 4. For any given quantity, producers now require $4 more to supply it; that wedge is the tax.

7. Of the $4 per-unit excise tax, consumers bear:

Correct: 75%. Consumer burden = new price − old price = $15 − $12 = $3. Producer burden = $4 − $3 = $1 (they net $11, down from $12). Consumer share = $3 ÷ $4 = 75%.

8. The excess burden (deadweight loss) of this excise tax equals:

Correct: 2. \(\text{DWL} = \tfrac{1}{2} \times \text{tax} \times \Delta Q = \tfrac{1}{2} \times 4 \times (4-3) = 2\). Tax revenue = $4 × 3 = $12. The DWL is the Harberger triangle — welfare lost that nobody captures.

9. A taxpayer's marginal rate is 35% and average rate is 22%. A $2,000 salary increase raises their tax bill by:

Correct: $700. The marginal rate applies to the next dollar of income. Extra tax = $2,000 × 35% = $700. The average rate (22%) is irrelevant here — it describes the average burden on existing income, not incremental income.

10. A $0.60 per-gallon petrol tax collects $600 from a household earning $20,000 and $600 from a household earning $120,000. This tax structure is:

Correct: Regressive. Avg rate for $20k household = $600 ÷ $20,000 = 3.0%. Avg rate for $120k household = $600 ÷ $120,000 = 0.5%. The rate falls as income rises → regressive.
Section B — National Income Accounting (Ch 21)

11. Given the data below, calculate NNP at Factor Cost:

ItemValue ($B)
Net Domestic Product at Factor Cost15,480
Net earned income from abroad80
Indirect taxes1,150
Subsidies190
Correct: $15,560B. NNP at FC = NDP at FC + net factor income from abroad = $15,480 + $80 = $15,560B. This converts domestic (within borders) to national (by nationals) by adding net earnings from abroad.

12. Using the same data, NNP at Market Prices is:

Correct: $16,520B. NNP at MP = NNP at FC + Indirect Taxes − Subsidies = $15,560 + $1,150 − $190 = $16,520B.

14. Using 2016 as base year, Real GDP for 2017 is (2016 prices: sugar cane $0.80, yo-yos $2.50, rum $10, peanuts $2.00, harmonicas $25, peanut butter $4.50 — 2017 quantities: 180, 700, 160, 225, 75, 85):

Correct: $6,201.50. 180×0.80 + 700×2.50 + 160×10 + 225×2 + 75×25 + 85×4.50 = 144 + 1,750 + 1,600 + 450 + 1,875 + 382.50 = $6,201.50. $5,969 is real GDP for 2016 itself.

15. Real GDP 2016 (base 2016) = $5,969; Real GDP 2017 (base 2016) = $6,201.50. Growth rate 2016 → 2017:

Correct: 3.90%. \(\frac{6201.5 - 5969}{5969} \times 100 = \frac{232.5}{5969} \times 100 \approx 3.90\%\). The 4.08% distractor uses 2017 as base year — the choice of base year changes the measured growth rate.

16. Using 2017 as base year, Real GDP for 2018 is (2017 prices: sugar cane $1.00, yo-yos $3.00, rum $12, peanuts $2.50, harmonicas $30, peanut butter $4.50 — 2018 quantities same as Q13):

Correct: $8,130.00. 225×1 + 750×3 + 180×12 + 225×2.50 + 85×30 + 85×4.50 = 225 + 2,250 + 2,160 + 562.50 + 2,550 + 382.50 = $8,130. Differs from $6,812.50 (base 2016) because 2017 prices are higher.

17. Real GDP 2017 (base 2016) = $6,201.50; Real GDP 2018 (base 2016) = $6,812.50. Growth rate 2017 → 2018:

Correct: 9.85%. \(\frac{6812.5 - 6201.5}{6201.5} \times 100 = \frac{611}{6201.5} \times 100 \approx 9.85\%\). Using 2017 as base gives 9.94% — both are valid; chain-weighting averages them.
Section C — GDP Applied (Ch 21)

18. A wheat farmer sells wheat to a miller for $0.50. The miller sells flour to a baker for $0.90. The baker sells bread to consumers for $1.50. The contribution to GDP from this production chain is:

Correct: $1.50. GDP counts only final goods to avoid double-counting. Alternatively, sum value-added at each stage: farmer adds $0.50, miller adds $0.40, baker adds $0.60 — total = $1.50. Both methods give the same answer.

19. Given: C = $9,500B, I = $1,800B, G = $2,200B, Exports = $600B, Imports = $850B. GDP = ?

Correct: $13,250B. GDP = C + I + G + (EX − IM) = 9,500 + 1,800 + 2,200 + (600 − 850) = 13,500 − 250 = $13,250B. Net exports are negative here because imports exceed exports.

20. GNP = $16,000B; Capital Consumption Allowance (depreciation) = $1,200B. NNP = ?

Correct: $14,800B. NNP = GNP − Capital Consumption Allowance = $16,000 − $1,200 = $14,800B. Subtracting depreciation converts gross (before wear and tear) to net (after).

21. Nominal GDP = $12,000B; Real GDP (base year) = $10,000B. The GDP deflator equals:

Correct: 120. \(\text{Deflator} = \frac{\text{Nominal GDP}}{\text{Real GDP}} \times 100 = \frac{12{,}000}{10{,}000} \times 100 = 120\). A deflator of 120 means the price level is 20% above the base year.

22. Nominal GDP rises from $5,000B to $6,000B while the GDP deflator rises from 100 to 125. Real GDP in the new year is:

Correct: $4,800B. Real GDP = (Nominal ÷ Deflator) × 100 = ($6,000 ÷ 125) × 100 = $4,800B. Real output fell even though nominal GDP rose — all the nominal gain was inflation.

23. Which of the following is NOT counted in GDP?

Correct: Social Security payments. Transfer payments — Social Security, unemployment benefits, welfare — are excluded from GDP because they do not represent payment for current production of goods or services. They simply redistribute income.
Section D — Externalities & Macroeconomics (Ch 16 & 20)

24. In the optimal-pollution diagram, the socially efficient level of pollution is found where:

Correct: MSB of abatement = MSC of abatement. Zero pollution is not efficient because the last unit of pollution reduction costs more than it's worth. Efficiency requires the marginal benefit of cleaning up to equal the marginal cost of doing so.

25. A Pigouvian tax equal to the marginal external cost corrects a negative externality by:

Correct: supply curve shifts left. The tax raises the firm's private marginal cost by the marginal external cost, making the private supply curve coincide with the MSC curve. The demand curve is unaffected.

26. Firm A can cut emissions at $10/tonne; Firm B at $30/tonne. Under cap-and-trade, economic efficiency requires:

Correct: Firm A reduces, sells to B. Cap-and-trade achieves the emissions target at lowest total cost by concentrating abatement in the cheapest firm. Any permit price between $10 and $30 makes it profitable for Firm A to cut and sell, and for Firm B to buy rather than abate.

27. At the unregulated market equilibrium, Marginal Social Cost (MSC) exceeds Marginal Private Cost (MC). This implies:

Correct: negative externality, over-production. MSC > MC means the true social cost of each unit exceeds what the firm accounts for — an unpriced cost is imposed on third parties. The firm produces beyond the efficient quantity where MSC = MSB.

28. Labor force = 150 million; Employed = 142.5 million. The unemployment rate is:

Correct: 5.0%. Unemployed = 150 − 142.5 = 7.5 million. Rate = 7.5 ÷ 150 × 100 = 5.0%. The 7.5% distractor confuses the number of unemployed (7.5M) with the rate; 95% is the employment rate, not unemployment rate.

29. Real GDP in Q1 = $18,000B, Q2 = $17,800B, Q3 = $17,500B, Q4 = $17,200B. This pattern is most consistent with:

Correct: Recession. GDP has declined for four consecutive quarters — well beyond the standard two-quarter definition of a recession. A trough would be a single low point, not a sustained decline.

30. A payroll tax is imposed on employers. Labor supply is perfectly inelastic. The wage received by workers will:

Correct: workers bear the full burden. With a vertical (perfectly inelastic) labor supply, quantity can't change. The tax shifts labor demand down; to clear the market at the same quantity, take-home wages fall by exactly the full tax. Legal incidence is irrelevant — economic incidence follows elasticities.

31. Two goods face the same per-unit tax. Good A has perfectly inelastic demand; Good B has elastic demand. The deadweight loss is larger for:

Correct: Good B. DWL = ½ × tax × ΔQ. Perfectly inelastic demand means ΔQ = 0, so DWL = 0 for Good A. Elastic demand produces a large ΔQ, so DWL is large for Good B. This is why taxing inelastic goods (gasoline, cigarettes) raises revenue with minimal efficiency cost.

32. In the circular flow model, government spending on goods and services is best described as:

Correct: an injection. Government spending enters the product market as demand for output — it injects purchasing power into the circular flow. Taxes are the corresponding leakage (pulling funds out of household income).

33. Vertical equity in taxation requires that:

Correct: higher ability → higher burden. Vertical equity is the principle that people in different economic positions should be treated differently — specifically that those with more resources should pay more. Horizontal equity (equal treatment of equals) is the separate concept in option A.

34. Arrow's Impossibility Theorem demonstrates that:

Correct: no voting rule satisfies all criteria. Arrow proved that any voting rule that satisfies basic fairness conditions (unanimity, independence of irrelevant alternatives, non-dictatorship) will sometimes produce intransitive social rankings — cycling preferences with no stable majority winner.

35. The Keynesian revolution argued that during a recession, the appropriate policy response is:

Correct: expansionary fiscal policy. Keynes argued that wages and prices are sticky downward, so markets can remain depressed indefinitely. Government must step in to raise aggregate demand directly — through spending increases or tax cuts — to return the economy to full employment.
